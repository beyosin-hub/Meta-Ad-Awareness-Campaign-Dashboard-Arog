# Meta-Ad-Awareness-Campaign-Dashboard-Arog
Meta Ads Awareness campaign dashboard and performance insights for Arog Global Private Limited.
Campaign Duration: 2 Days
Objective: Brand Awareness
Client: Arog Global Private Limited
Total Ad Spend: ₹118 (for 2 days)
The campaign achieved significant reach and visibility at a very low cost, indicating strong cost efficiency.

## Objective
To analyze the performance of a Meta Ads awareness campaign and evaluate reach, impressions, and engagement efficiency from specied target audience.

- From canva created the carousel content on "Why A2 Gir Cow Ghee is costlier than any other Ghee"

## Insights
**Reach & Impressions**
Total Reach: 71,000
Total Impressions: 72,000
**Difference Between Reach and Impressions**
Reach refers to the number of unique users who saw the ad at least once.
Impressions represent the total number of times the ad was shown, including repeat views by the same users.
The close gap between reach and impressions indicates low ad fatigue and controlled exposure.

**Gender-wise Performance**
Male reach is higher than female reach
This suggests that the ad content or targeting resonated more strongly with male users.
Age-wise Reach Analysis
Highest Reach: 18–24 age group
Target vs Actual Performance
The original target audience was 25–54, and performance within this group was as follows:
Age Group	Reach
25–34 -	25.5K
35–44 -	9.6K
45–54 -	3K
Among the target audience, 25–34 emerged as the strongest performing segment.

**Cost Efficiency**
Cost per Result: ₹1.75
This indicates that the campaign delivered awareness at a very economical cost, making it highly cost-effective.

**Engagement Metrics (Age 25–34)**
Link Clicks: 5.00
Shows moderate user intent within the highest-performing target age group.

**Frequency**
Frequency: 4.05
1. What is Frequency?
-> Frequency represents the average number of times a single user saw the ad.
2. Why Frequency is Useful:
-> Helps measure ad repetition
A frequency of 4.05 is healthy for awareness campaigns.

**CTR (Click-Through Rate)**
CTR: 0.13%
1. What is CTR?
-> CTR measures the percentage of users who clicked the ad after seeing it.
2. Why CTR is Useful:
-> Indicates ad relevance
Measures how compelling the creative and messaging are
For an awareness campaign, this CTR is acceptable, as the primary goal is visibility, not clicks.

**CPM (Cost Per 1,000 Impressions)**
CPM: ₹6.04
1. What is CPM?
-> CPM represents the cost to show the ad 1,000 times.
2. Why CPM is Useful:
-> Measures cost efficiency
Lower CPM = broader reach at lower cost
A CPM of ₹6.04 is extremely efficient, indicating strong ad delivery optimization.

## Conclusion
Campaign Performance Verdict: POSITIVE
1. Extremely low ad spend with high reach
2. Strong performance in 25–34 age group
3. Efficient CPM and cost per result
4. Healthy frequency ensuring brand recall
5. Wide geographic coverage across major states

**Business Recommendations**
1. Focus more on age group 25–34
→ Highest reach, engagement, and efficiency
2. Optimize creatives for male audience
→ Higher response observed
3. Increase budget gradually
→ Given low CPM, scaling can significantly increase awareness

**Run follow-up campaigns**
Traffic or Lead campaigns targeting 25–34
Retarget users exposed during awareness phase
